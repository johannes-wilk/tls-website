What We Do

Terrestrial Laser Scanning (TLS)

  • • We use two state-of-the-art ground-based laser scanners: Riegl VZ400i and VZ600i.
  • • Data is collected following standard grid or RINGO protocols in forest plots.
  • • TLS allows us to capture detailed 3D representations of complex vegetation structures.
  • • Our equipment is capable of handling both small and extensive plots in challenging environments.
  • • This method delivers high-resolution point clouds that enable structural forest analysis.

UAV Lidar

  • • We operate two Riegl lidar systems: VUX-120 and MiniVUX.
  • • VUX-120 enables high-detail scanning, ideal for precision studies.
  • • MiniVUX offers extended flight time with slightly reduced resolution for broader coverage.
  • • Two UAV platforms allow us to flexibly adapt to mission scale and terrain.
  • • UAV lidar is crucial for rapid and large-scale data acquisition across diverse forest types.

Postprocessing

  • • We register, align, and filter point clouds for maximum clarity and usability.
  • • High-resolution terrain models are produced: DTM, DSM, and DEM.
  • • We apply tree segmentation algorithms for individual tree detection and structure extraction.
  • • Structural metrics are derived to support forest monitoring, biomass analysis, and carbon accounting.
  • • All processing steps follow reproducible scientific protocols.

Field Sites

Las Majadas, Spain

Las Majadas is a Mediterranean savanna ecosystem (dehesa) characterized by scattered oak trees with an understory of grassland. Our campaign in December 2024 covered 65 hectares using Terrestrial Laser Scanning to capture the unique structure of this open woodland system. The site is part of the ICOS (Integrated Carbon Observation System) network and provides valuable data on carbon fluxes in semi-arid environments.

Jenaro Herrera, Peru

Jenaro Herrera represents a pristine tropical rainforest ecosystem in the Peruvian Amazon. Our November 2024 campaign focused on capturing the complex vertical structure of this highly diverse forest. Using Terrestrial Laser Scanning across 4 hectares, we documented the multi-layered canopy and understory vegetation. This site is particularly valuable for understanding carbon storage in undisturbed tropical forests and serves as a reference for forest degradation studies.

Tharandt, Germany

Tharandt Forest is a managed coniferous forest in eastern Germany that serves as an important research site for forestry and ecosystem studies. Our January 2025 campaign covered 3 hectares using Terrestrial Laser Scanning to document the structure of this temperate forest dominated by Norway spruce and Scots pine. This site is particularly valuable for studying forest management impacts on carbon sequestration and provides a comparison point for natural versus managed forest structures.

Hohes Holz, Germany

Hohes Holz is a deciduous forest in central Germany that serves as a key research site for understanding carbon dynamics in temperate forests. Our April 2024 campaign used Terrestrial Laser Scanning to document 3 hectares of this mixed beech-oak forest. The site is equipped with an eddy covariance flux tower that continuously measures carbon exchange between the forest and atmosphere, making it an ideal location for validating structural metrics derived from TLS data against direct carbon flux measurements.
